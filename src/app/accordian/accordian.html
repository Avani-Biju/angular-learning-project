Every Indian state has a capital city that serves as its administrative headquarters. It is where the state government, legislative assembly, and major government offices function to manage public administration.

India consists of 28 states and 8 union territories, each with its own administrative structure. Every state has a designated capital city, while union territories are governed according to their constitutional provisions.

Capital cities are centers of governance, administration, education, and culture. They house important government institutions and play a key role in the political, economic, and social development of their respective states.
