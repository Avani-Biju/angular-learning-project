A capital city is the city where a country's government is located. It often serves as the political, administrative, and cultural center of the nation. Examples include New Delhi (India), London (United Kingdom), and Tokyo (Japan).

New Delhi is the capital of India. It is home to important government institutions including the Parliament of India, Rashtrapati Bhavan, and the Supreme Court. The city plays a vital role in the country's administration and decision-making.

Learning about capital cities helps us understand geography, culture, history, and international relations. Capital cities are often centers of government, tourism, education, and economic activity, making them important places around the world.
